What it is

The Okanagan Basin Water Board maintains a continuously-updated mirror of the hydrometric data for the Okanagan watershed. It brings together Water Survey of Canada gauges, BC provincial / Okanagan Nation Alliance–serviced stations and the provincial groundwater observation well network into one queryable archive, with every value tagged by its source, quality grade and approval level.

It exists as a resilience layer: if access to the provincial data warehouse is ever interrupted, OBWB's forecasting, drought and water-supply tools keep running from this backup.

How it works

🇨🇦 Sovereign by design

Stored in PostgreSQL + PostGIS on Google Cloud, Montréal — all data resident in Canada.

🔄 Always current

Automated jobs pull real-time data daily and run a weekly reconciliation audit that backfills any gaps against the source of record.

📏 Full provenance

Discharge, stage, water temperature and groundwater level — each point carries its grade, approval level and ingest history, plus rating and station metadata.

📚 Deep history

Daily records reach back to 1915 (discharge) and 1943 (water level), with groundwater levels to the 1960s, alongside the live real-time feed.

Access the data

The data is open. Explore it and download it from the live dashboard:

  • Interactive dashboard — summary, station map, freshness, groundwater wells, ONA station audit, and pipeline status
  • CSV downloads — stations, coverage, latest readings and the full daily record (on the dashboard's Data Access tab)
  • Read-only database access for analysts (SQL / R / Python) — available on request from OBWB Water Stewardship

Data sources: Water Survey of Canada (Environment and Climate Change Canada) and the BC Ministry of Environment, as available. Please credit the original source in any reuse.
